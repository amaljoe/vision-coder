PILLARS

The CREATE H2O program includes five pillars offering in-depth training.

Within each of these pillars, there are many options from which students may choose. For each student accepted into the program, the pillar requirements are tailored to the individual student's educational background, degree course requirements, and the focus area the student wishes to pursue in First Nations water and sanitation security.

Each student accepted into the program is assigned two professors who will act as mentors. Along with the CREATE H2O program co-ordinator, these professors will assist students in designing the pillar structure and activities that best match each student's career path and goals.

Students accepted into the program will receive financial support consisting of a stipend and travel awards.

DESIGNING YOUR PROGRAM TO OBTAIN A CO-CURRICULAR RECORD IN FIRST NATIONS WATER AND SANITATION SECURITY

Undergraduate students must choose between a relevant research-based term (Pillar 1) and a professional work term (Pillar 5). In addition, undergraduate students are required to complete 8 portfolio units across at least two of the other three program pillars before you graduate.

Pillar Main Task Notes
Pillar 1 - Research project and dissertation under the supervision of more than one university professor with water/sanitation systems knowledge. Courses, workshops, conference presentations All students will be required to present work at the annual summer CREATE H2O conference. (The first one is June 2014 and is at the University of Manitoba.)
Pillar 2 - Water science and watershed systems knowledge. Courses, workshops, conference presentations Courses already completed by students as part of their degree program may qualify. See approved undergraduate and graduate course list worksheets.
Pillar 3 - Indigenous theory, law and policy. Courses, workshops, conference presentations Courses already completed by students as part of their degree program may qualify. See approved undergraduate and graduate course list worksheets.

AN EXAMPLE OF AN UNDERGRADUATE STUDENT ENROLLED IN THE CREATE H2O PROGRAM

  • Summer research project under the leadership of professors at the University of Manitoba or Trent University (pillar 1).
  • One water science course from approved list (pillar 2): 3 units.
  • One law science course from approved list (pillar 3): 3 units.
  • Present H2O research at conference (pillar 2 or 3): 2 units.

AN EXAMPLE OF A GRADUATE STUDENT ENROLLED IN THE CREATE H2O PROGRAM

  • Research thesis under the leadership of professors at the University of Manitoba or Trent University (pillar 1).
  • This science course: an approved list (pillar 3): 3 units.
  • Present H2O research at conference (pillar 2 or 3): 2 units.
